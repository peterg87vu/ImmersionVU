Immersion by Peter Gillis

The Melbourne Sandbelt

There’s something extra special about the sand belt golf courses in Melbourne Victoria, Australia. They are natural like a Links course, but with a Parkland quality. There is something about them, so impressive and inspiring. The turf is firm, fast, and always in perfect condition. The greens firm, almost impossible to penetrate the surface with rarely a need to fix pitch marks. It’s the type of sand that packs down the top surface like clay, it’s so firm, allowing the bunkers to be dug into the edges of the greens with the green surfaces all the way to those bunker faces so unique and impressive. Dig down a few feet and you come to the natural sand making the bunkers so ideal with sharp edges, never a plugged lie and just the right amount of sand at the base. The other unique quality of all the sand-belt courses are the diversity of the hole styles, just classic old golden era design, Mackenzie style bunkering, natural rolling terrain, spectacular flowering gums add to the look and feel. The par threes in the sand belt are the best anywhere in the world. It seems every course has a collection of the world‘s greatest par three’s. Royal Melbourne, Kingston Heath, Victoria in fact all the courses have just spectacular short to mid length par threes. So memorable. Also many short par four’s along with a mix of extraordinarily difficult four’s. Just the right mix of both. The sand-belt is definitely the best area for a golf trip anywhere in the world.
— Ian Baker Finch, 1991 Open Champion
